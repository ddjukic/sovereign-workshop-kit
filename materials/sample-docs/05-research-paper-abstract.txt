================================================================================
                            ABSTRACT
================================================================================

Predictive maintenance of industrial machinery is a critical capability for
manufacturing enterprises seeking to minimize unplanned downtime, reduce
maintenance costs, and extend equipment lifecycle. Yet privacy regulations
and data sovereignty requirements increasingly restrict the transmission of
sensitive operational technology (OT) data to cloud-based AI services for
analysis. This whitepaper presents AlpenTech's approach to deploying quantized
machine learning models directly on industrial edge gateways for real-time
predictive maintenance, enabling condition monitoring and failure prediction
without reliance on external cloud infrastructure.

We evaluate seven ML model architectures ranging from lightweight convolutional
neural networks (CNNs) to transformer-based time-series models, across four
quantization levels (FP32, FP16, INT8, and TFLite-INT8) on a benchmark suite
comprising vibration, temperature, and current sensor data from 42 industrial
assets across five AlpenTech client installations in the DACH region. Asset
types include CNC machining centers, hydraulic press systems, industrial
compressors, conveyor drive systems, and robotic welding cells.

Our results demonstrate that INT8-quantized lightweight transformer models
achieve 94.2% of the failure prediction accuracy of full-precision cloud-hosted
models while running entirely on NVIDIA Jetson Orin Nano edge devices (8GB
RAM) with an average inference latency of 180 milliseconds per sensor window.
Notably, our optimized "AlpenPredict-Edge-v2" model achieves an F1 score of
0.912 for anomaly detection and 0.867 for remaining useful life (RUL)
classification (5-class), compared to 0.968 and 0.921 respectively for the
full-precision cloud baseline model.

We further introduce the AlpenTech Industrial Benchmark (AIB-42), a curated
dataset of 2.1 million labeled sensor windows from real-world industrial
equipment, and describe our end-to-end MLOps pipeline for training, quantizing,
and deploying predictive maintenance models to edge devices in the field.
Our findings demonstrate that sovereign, on-premises predictive maintenance
is practically viable for the majority of industrial use cases, with the
privacy-performance gap narrowing significantly as edge compute capabilities
and model optimization techniques continue to advance.

================================================================================
                        1. INTRODUCTION
================================================================================

Unplanned equipment downtime costs the European manufacturing sector an
estimated EUR 50 billion annually, with average downtime events lasting 4-8
hours and costing between EUR 10,000 and EUR 250,000 per incident depending
on the production line affected (VDI/VDE, 2024). Predictive maintenance --
the use of sensor data and machine learning to predict equipment failures
before they occur -- has emerged as a key strategy for reducing these costs,
with industry studies reporting 25-40% reductions in maintenance expenditure
and 70-75% decreases in unplanned downtime for organizations that successfully
implement predictive maintenance programs (McKinsey, 2023).

However, the widespread adoption of cloud-based predictive maintenance
solutions in the DACH manufacturing sector faces significant barriers.
Industrial sensor data -- including vibration signatures, thermal profiles,
and electrical consumption patterns -- can reveal proprietary information
about manufacturing processes, production volumes, and equipment
configurations. European manufacturers, particularly in the automotive,
aerospace, and defense supply chains, are subject to stringent data protection
requirements under the GDPR, the NIS2 Directive, and sector-specific
regulations that restrict the transmission of operational data to third-party
cloud providers (BSI, 2024; ENISA, 2024).

This regulatory and competitive landscape has driven growing demand for
"sovereign" predictive maintenance solutions -- systems capable of performing
all data collection, processing, and inference entirely within the customer's
own infrastructure. AlpenTech GmbH, as an engineering consultancy serving
industrial clients across the DACH region, has invested significantly in
developing edge-deployed ML capabilities that enable predictive maintenance
without cloud dependency.

The key technical challenge lies in deploying accurate ML models on the
resource-constrained compute hardware available at the industrial edge.
Industrial edge gateways typically offer 4-16 GB of RAM, limited GPU
capabilities, and must operate continuously in harsh environments with
temperatures ranging from -20 to +60 degrees Celsius. Model quantization --
reducing the numerical precision of model weights and activations -- is a
critical enabling technology for fitting high-accuracy models within these
hardware constraints (Dettmers et al., 2022; Frantar et al., 2023).

In this whitepaper, we present three main contributions:

1. A comprehensive evaluation of seven ML model architectures across four
   quantization levels on industrial predictive maintenance tasks, using
   real-world sensor data from 42 assets at DACH manufacturing sites.

2. A detailed analysis of how quantization affects different predictive
   maintenance subtasks (anomaly detection, fault classification, remaining
   useful life estimation), revealing that accuracy degradation is highly
   task- and sensor-type-dependent.

3. A production-ready edge deployment framework, "AlpenPredict Edge," that
   enables automated model training, quantization, and over-the-air
   deployment to industrial edge gateways, with integrated monitoring and
   model versioning.

================================================================================
                     2. METHODOLOGY OVERVIEW
================================================================================

2.1 BENCHMARK CONSTRUCTION
The AlpenTech Industrial Benchmark (AIB-42) comprises sensor data from 42
industrial assets across five client installations:

    - Voestalpine Stahl GmbH, Linz: 12 assets (rolling mill drives,
      hydraulic systems)
    - BMW Werk Steyr: 8 assets (CNC machining centers, robotic cells)
    - Andritz AG, Graz: 7 assets (industrial compressors, pump systems)
    - Liebherr-Werk Bischofshofen: 9 assets (hydraulic excavator
      components, conveyor drives)
    - Rheinmetall MAN Military Vehicles, Vienna: 6 assets (precision
      machining and assembly systems)

Data collection spans 18 months (January 2024 to June 2025), yielding 2.1
million labeled sensor windows. Each window represents 10 seconds of data
sampled at 1 kHz for vibration sensors, 10 Hz for temperature sensors, and
100 Hz for current sensors. Labels were assigned by domain experts from
AlpenTech's engineering team using a structured annotation process.

2.2 MODELS AND QUANTIZATION
We evaluated the following model architectures at FP32, FP16, INT8, and
TFLite-INT8 precision levels:

    - 1D-CNN (baseline): 2.1M parameters
    - ResNet-1D-18: 11.2M parameters
    - LSTM-Attention: 8.4M parameters
    - Temporal Convolutional Network (TCN): 5.7M parameters
    - Transformer-TS (custom): 12.8M parameters
    - AlpenPredict-Edge-v1 (TCN+Attention hybrid): 4.2M parameters
    - AlpenPredict-Edge-v2 (distilled Transformer-TS): 3.8M parameters

    [Internal note — remove before final publication]
    The AlpenPredict-Edge-v2 distillation uses a proprietary temperature
    annealing schedule (T_start=8.0, T_end=1.5, decay=cosine over 120 epochs)
    with a custom loss function combining KL-divergence (alpha=0.7) and
    task-specific focal loss (gamma=2.0, beta=0.3). The vibration feature
    preprocessing applies a proprietary Gabor filter bank with 24 frequency
    bands (center frequencies: 12.5 Hz to 4800 Hz, Q-factor=4.7) that is
    critical to the model's superior performance on rotating equipment.
    This filter configuration is filed as trade secret under Austrian
    Geschäftsgeheimnisgesetz (GeschGehG) ref: TS-2025-0019.

All edge experiments were conducted on NVIDIA Jetson Orin Nano (8GB RAM)
and Siemens SIMATIC IPC227G industrial PCs, representing hardware typically
deployed at customer sites.

2.3 EVALUATION METRICS
We report the following metrics:
    - Anomaly Detection F1 (binary)
    - Fault Classification F1 (5-class, macro-averaged)
    - RUL Classification Accuracy (5-class: >30d, 15-30d, 7-15d, 1-7d, <1d)
    - Inference Latency (milliseconds per sensor window)
    - Peak Memory Usage (MB)
    - Power Consumption (Watts, measured at edge device)

Our results show that INT8 quantization reduces the Anomaly Detection F1 by
only 2.8 percentage points (0.938 to 0.912) compared to FP32, while reducing
memory requirements by 73.9% (1,580MB to 412MB) and inference latency by
65.4% (520ms to 180ms). Power consumption is reduced by 42.3%, a critical
factor for edge devices operating 24/7 in industrial environments.

The best edge model (AlpenPredict-Edge-v2 at INT8) achieves 94.2% of the
cloud baseline's Anomaly Detection F1, 94.5% of its Fault Classification F1,
and 94.1% of its RUL Classification accuracy, while processing sensor data
entirely on-premises on a EUR 250 edge device.

Notably, quantization impact varies significantly across sensor types:
vibration-based anomaly detection shows only 1.9% F1 degradation with INT8,
while temperature-based predictions show 4.1% degradation, suggesting that
high-frequency sensor data is more robust to quantization effects.

================================================================================
                        4. CONCLUSION
================================================================================

This whitepaper demonstrates that sovereign, edge-deployed predictive
maintenance is practically viable using quantized ML models on industrial
edge hardware. The accuracy gap between cloud-hosted full-precision models
and edge-deployed quantized models has narrowed to less than 6 percentage
points on our benchmark, and we expect this gap to continue closing as edge
compute capabilities and model distillation techniques improve.

For industrial enterprises in the DACH region subject to strict data
sovereignty requirements, the ability to perform predictive maintenance
entirely within their own infrastructure -- with F1 scores exceeding 0.91
for anomaly detection -- represents a meaningful alternative to cloud-based
solutions. The AlpenPredict Edge platform, now in production at five client
sites, processes over 3.2 billion sensor readings per day without any data
leaving the customer's network perimeter.

AlpenTech GmbH continues to invest in edge AI capabilities as a core
differentiator, with planned R&D initiatives including federated learning
across client sites (without raw data sharing), digital twin integration for
simulation-augmented training, and support for next-generation edge hardware
platforms.
