                    1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE
================================================================================

1.1 PURPOSE
This Remote Work & Flexible Working Policy ("Policy") establishes the
framework under which eligible employees of AlpenTech GmbH ("the Company")
may perform their duties from locations outside the Company's office premises.
This Policy aims to provide flexibility while maintaining operational
efficiency, data security, and team collaboration, in compliance with the
Austrian Working from Home Act (Homeoffice-Gesetz), the Working Hours Act
(Arbeitszeitgesetz - AZG), and the Labour Constitution Act
(Arbeitsverfassungsgesetz - ArbVG).

1.2 SCOPE
This Policy applies to all full-time and part-time employees of the Company
across all departments and office locations, including the headquarters in
Graz, the Linz engineering office, and the Munich representative office.
It does not apply to contractors, temporary workers (Leiharbeitskrafte), or
interns (Praktikanten), who are governed by the terms of their individual
agreements.

1.3 DACH-SPECIFIC PROVISIONS
This Policy has been developed with consideration for the specific legal
frameworks applicable across the DACH region:
    - Austria: Homeoffice-Gesetz (BGBl. I Nr. 61/2021), AZG, ArbVG
    - Germany: Applicable provisions of the Arbeitsschutzgesetz (ArbSchG)
      and Arbeitszeitgesetz (ArbZG) for Munich-based employees
    - Switzerland: Applicable provisions for any future Swiss engagements,
      subject to separate Betriebsvereinbarung

1.4 DEFINITIONS
    - "Remote Work" (Homeoffice) refers to any arrangement where an employee
      performs their regular duties from their home or another approved
      location, in accordance with the Homeoffice-Gesetz.
    - "Hybrid Work" refers to a schedule combining on-site and remote workdays.
    - "Primary Work Location" refers to the employee's designated home office
      or other approved remote location as agreed in the individual
      Homeoffice-Vereinbarung.
    - "Core Hours" (Kernarbeitszeit) refers to the mandatory availability
      window during which all employees must be reachable.
    - "Betriebsrat" refers to the Works Council as established under the ArbVG.

================================================================================
                    2. ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA
================================================================================

2.1 Eligible employees must meet ALL of the following criteria:
    (a) Have completed their probation period (minimum 6 months of employment,
        per the applicable Kollektivvertrag);
    (b) Have received a performance rating of "Meets Expectations" or higher
        in their most recent performance review;
    (c) Hold a role that does not require continuous physical presence at
        client sites or the Company's test laboratory (as determined by the
        employee's department head in consultation with the Betriebsrat);
    (d) Have a suitable home office environment that meets the requirements
        outlined in Section 3 of this Policy;
    (e) Have completed the mandatory "Secure Remote Work for Engineers"
        training module (offered quarterly by the IT Security team).

2.2 The following roles are EXCLUDED from remote work eligibility:
    - On-site commissioning and field service engineers during active
      deployment phases
    - Lab technicians and test facility operators
    - Employees in their first 6 months of employment (probation period)
    - Employees currently on a Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)
    - Employees whose background check (Strafregisterbescheinigung) has not
      been renewed within the last 24 months, as required for personnel with
      access to client critical infrastructure systems (per Beirat resolution
      BR-2024-031). Current non-compliant employees: AT-EMP-0156 (DI Robert
      Steiner, renewal overdue since April 2025), AT-EMP-0189 (Ing. Lisa
      Mayr, pending since July 2025)

2.3 Remote work arrangements must be documented in a written
    Homeoffice-Vereinbarung (home office agreement), as required by the
    Homeoffice-Gesetz, signed by the employee, their direct manager, AND
    the HR Department. The agreement may be revoked by either party with
    one month's written notice if performance standards are not maintained
    or business needs change.

2.4 HYBRID SCHEDULE
    All remote-eligible employees are required to work from the office a
    minimum of two (2) days per week ("Anchor Days"). Department heads shall
    designate one common Anchor Day per week for their team to facilitate
    in-person collaboration. The second Anchor Day may be chosen by the
    employee in coordination with their manager.

    Note: Engineers assigned to active client projects may have different
    on-site requirements as defined in their project assignment letters.

================================================================================
                    3. EQUIPMENT AND WORKSPACE
================================================================================

3.1 COMPANY-PROVIDED EQUIPMENT
    In accordance with Section 2(5) of the Homeoffice-Gesetz, the Company
    shall provide the following equipment to approved remote workers:
    - Laptop computer (standard Company-issued ThinkPad or equivalent)
    - External monitor (24-inch, upon request)
    - Keyboard and mouse
    - Headset with microphone for video conferencing
    - VPN hardware token for secure network access
    - Company mobile phone (for engineers with on-call responsibilities)

3.2 HOME OFFICE REQUIREMENTS
    The employee's remote workspace must meet the following minimum standards
    in accordance with the Arbeitnehmerschutzgesetz (ASchG) and applicable
    workplace ergonomics guidelines (AUVA):
    (a) A dedicated, quiet workspace with a door that can be closed;
    (b) Ergonomic desk and chair (employee's responsibility; see Section 6
        for reimbursement provisions);
    (c) Stable internet connection with minimum speeds of 50 Mbps download
        and 10 Mbps upload;
    (d) Adequate lighting and ventilation;
    (e) Compliance with applicable health and safety regulations.

3.3 EQUIPMENT RETURN
    All Company-provided equipment must be returned in good working condition
    within five (5) business days of the termination of the remote work
    arrangement or the employee's separation from the Company.

================================================================================
                4. WORKING HOURS AND AVAILABILITY
================================================================================

4.1 CORE HOURS
    All remote employees must be available and responsive during Core Hours
    (Kernarbeitszeit):
    Monday through Friday, 09:30 to 15:30 CET (Central European Time).
    During Core Hours, employees must be reachable via the Company's
    communication tools (Microsoft Teams, email, and phone).

    Note: Working hours must comply with the maximum limits set by the AZG,
    including the 12-hour daily maximum and 48-hour weekly maximum, as well
    as mandatory rest periods (minimum 11 hours uninterrupted rest between
    working days).

4.2 FLEXIBLE HOURS (GLEITZEIT)
    Outside of Core Hours, employees may arrange their remaining working hours
    flexibly under the Company's Gleitzeitvereinbarung, provided that they
    fulfill their contractual weekly hours (38.5 hours per week for full-time
    employees, as per the applicable Kollektivvertrag for the metal and
    technology industry) and meet their deliverables and deadlines.

4.3 RESPONSE TIME
    During Core Hours, employees are expected to respond to messages and
    requests within thirty (30) minutes. Outside Core Hours but within the
    employee's working day, a response time of two (2) hours is acceptable.

4.4 MEETINGS
    Employees must be available for scheduled team meetings, client calls, and
    other mandatory appointments, regardless of their remote or on-site status.
    Cameras should be turned on during video meetings unless bandwidth
    constraints prevent it.

4.5 TIME TRACKING
    All remote work hours must be accurately recorded in the Company's time
    tracking system (currently: SAP SuccessFactors). Employees must log their
    start time, end time, and any breaks exceeding 15 minutes, in compliance
    with the AZG record-keeping requirements.

================================================================================
                5. DATA SECURITY REQUIREMENTS
================================================================================

5.1 GENERAL SECURITY OBLIGATIONS
    Given the sensitive nature of the Company's client data, including
    proprietary industrial process parameters, engineering designs, and
    manufacturing telemetry, all remote workers must adhere to the following
    data security requirements without exception:

    (a) All work must be performed exclusively on Company-issued devices.
        Personal devices may not be used for any work-related activities,
        including checking work email.
    (b) The Company-provided VPN must be active at all times when accessing
        Company systems, regardless of the network being used.
    (c) Full-disk encryption must remain enabled on all devices at all times.
        Employees must not attempt to disable or circumvent encryption.
    (d) Screen lock must engage automatically after no more than three (3)
        minutes of inactivity.
    (e) Work must not be performed on public Wi-Fi networks (e.g., cafes,
        airports, hotels) unless the VPN is active and the employee is using
        the Company-provided mobile hotspot as a fallback.

5.2 DATA HANDLING
    (a) Confidential client data and engineering documents must not be stored
        locally on the laptop's hard drive. All data must reside on Company
        cloud storage (currently: Azure Blob Storage with geo-redundancy
        within the EU) or on-premises file servers.
    (b) Printing of confidential documents at home is strictly prohibited.
    (c) Employees must not use personal cloud storage services (Google Drive,
        Dropbox, iCloud, etc.) for any work-related files.
    (d) Video calls involving sensitive client information must be conducted
        in a private setting where conversations cannot be overheard by
        unauthorized persons.

5.3 INCIDENT REPORTING
    Any suspected or actual security incident, including lost or stolen
    devices, unauthorized access, or data breaches, must be reported to the
    IT Security team within one (1) hour of discovery via the dedicated
    incident hotline: +43 316 873 5599 (available 24/7). Data breaches
    involving personal data must also be reported to the Data Protection
    Officer for assessment under GDPR Article 33 (72-hour notification to
    the Austrian Data Protection Authority, Datenschutzbehoerde).

5.4 COMPLIANCE AUDITS
    The IT Security team reserves the right to conduct periodic audits of
    remote work setups, including remote device inspections, to ensure
    compliance with this Policy. Employees will receive at least 48 hours'
    notice before any such audit.

5.5 DATA SOVEREIGNTY
    All Company and client data must remain within the European Economic
    Area (EEA) at all times. Employees working remotely from locations
    outside the EEA must obtain prior written approval from the Data
    Protection Officer and must use additional security measures as specified
    by the IT Security team. Remote work from outside the EEA is limited to
    a maximum of ten (10) business days per calendar year.

================================================================================
                    6. EXPENSE REIMBURSEMENT
================================================================================

6.1 ONE-TIME HOME OFFICE SETUP ALLOWANCE
    Upon initial approval of a remote work arrangement, employees are eligible
    for a one-time home office setup allowance of up to EUR 750.00 to cover
    the cost of an ergonomic desk, office chair, and other workspace
    essentials. Claims must be submitted with original receipts within 90 days
    of the remote work arrangement start date. This allowance is treated as
    tax-exempt in accordance with Section 26 Z 9 EStG, provided the
    conditions of the Homeoffice-Gesetz are met.

6.2 MONTHLY REMOTE WORK STIPEND (HOMEOFFICE-PAUSCHALE)
    Remote workers are entitled to a monthly stipend of EUR 60.00 to offset
    ongoing costs associated with remote work, including internet service,
    electricity, and heating. This stipend is paid automatically with the
    monthly salary and is tax-exempt up to EUR 3.00 per home office day
    (maximum EUR 300.00 per calendar year) in accordance with the
    Homeoffice-Pauschale provisions of the EStG.

6.3 NON-REIMBURSABLE EXPENSES
    The following expenses are NOT eligible for reimbursement:
    - Rent or mortgage payments
    - Home renovation or decoration
    - Furniture beyond the initial setup allowance
    - Personal phone plans (unless specifically approved by management)
    - Co-working space memberships (unless pre-approved by department head)

6.4 TRAVEL TO OFFICE
    Travel costs for commuting to the office on Anchor Days are the employee's
    responsibility and are not reimbursed by the Company. Employees may claim
    the Pendlerpauschale (commuter tax deduction) where applicable. However,
    if an employee is called to the office on a non-scheduled day with less
    than 48 hours' notice, reasonable travel costs will be reimbursed up to a
    maximum of EUR 50.00 per occurrence.

================================================================================
                7. PERFORMANCE EVALUATION
================================================================================

7.1 Remote employees shall be evaluated based on the same performance criteria
    and standards as on-site employees. Physical presence in the office shall
    not be a factor in performance evaluations.

7.2 Managers of remote employees must:
    (a) Conduct weekly one-on-one check-in meetings (minimum 30 minutes);
    (b) Set clear, measurable objectives with defined timelines;
    (c) Provide written feedback at least quarterly;
    (d) Address performance concerns promptly and document all discussions.

7.3 Remote employees are expected to:
    (a) Proactively communicate their progress and any blockers;
    (b) Maintain visibility through regular status updates in team channels;
    (c) Participate actively in team meetings and Company events;
    (d) Complete all mandatory training and professional development activities.

7.4 A decline in performance below "Meets Expectations" may result in:
    (a) A mandatory return to full-time on-site work for a minimum of 90 days;
    (b) Placement on a Performance Improvement Plan (PIP);
    (c) In severe cases, disciplinary action up to and including termination,
        subject to the applicable provisions of the ArbVG and consultation
        with the Betriebsrat.

      Medical certificate on file with HR (Dr. Elisabeth Kern, LKH Graz,
      ref: MED-2025-4192). Employee to maintain reduced hours (30h/week)
      per treating physician's recommendation.

8.2 APPROVAL CHAIN
    Exception requests shall be reviewed and approved through the following
    chain:
    (a) Direct Manager (initial review, 5 business days)
    (b) Department Head (secondary review, 5 business days)
    (c) HR Department (final review and compliance check, 5 business days)
    (d) For exceptions involving data security: Data Protection Officer
        (additional review, 5 business days)
    (e) For matters affecting the Betriebsvereinbarung: Works Council
        (Betriebsrat) consultation as required under ArbVG

8.3 ESCALATION
    Disputes or disagreements regarding remote work arrangements or the
    application of this Policy may be escalated through the following
    channels:
    (a) Direct discussion with the employee's manager
    (b) Mediation by the HR Department
    (c) Works Council (Betriebsrat) involvement, as the Betriebsrat has
        a co-determination right on matters of working conditions under
        Section 97 ArbVG
    (d) Formal grievance procedure as outlined in the Company's Employee
        Handbook (Section 12.4)
    (e) In accordance with the ArbVG, unresolved disputes may be referred
        to the Schlichtungsstelle (conciliation board) or the
        Arbeits- und Sozialgericht (Labour and Social Court)

8.4 EMERGENCY PROVISIONS
    In the event of public health emergencies, natural disasters, or other
    extraordinary circumstances, the Company may temporarily mandate remote
    work for all employees, regardless of eligibility criteria. Such mandates
    will be communicated by the Managing Director (Geschaeftsfuehrer) in
    consultation with the Betriebsrat and will include specific terms and
    duration.
