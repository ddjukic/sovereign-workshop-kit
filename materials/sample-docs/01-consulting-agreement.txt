                           RECITALS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

WHEREAS, the Client is an engineering consultancy specializing in industrial
automation, predictive maintenance, and engineering consulting services across
the DACH region, and desires to engage the Consultant to provide specialized
engineering and technical consulting services in connection with its
Industrie 4.0 client delivery program;

WHEREAS, the Consultant possesses specialized expertise in mechanical
engineering, control systems design, and industrial IoT integration, and is
willing to provide such services under the terms and conditions set forth in

contained herein, and for other good and valuable consideration, the receipt
and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the Parties agree as follows:

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
                    ARTICLE 1: SCOPE OF SERVICES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

1.1 The Consultant shall provide the following consulting services
    ("Services") to the Client:

    (a) Industrial Control Systems Assessment and Modernization Strategy
        Conduct a comprehensive assessment of the Client's customers'
        existing legacy PLC and SCADA infrastructure, comprising approximately
        38 production lines across 5 manufacturing sites, and develop a
        detailed modernization roadmap. This deliverable shall include a
        written report of no fewer than 80 pages, covering current state
        analysis, target architecture design, risk assessment, and a
        phased migration timeline.

    (b) Predictive Maintenance Data Pipeline Architecture
        Design and document a modern data pipeline architecture capable of
        processing industrial sensor telemetry data from the Client's
        customer installations, which currently amounts to approximately
        1.8 terabytes per day. The architecture shall support real-time
        streaming from edge gateways, batch processing for model training,
        and data quality monitoring. Deliverables include architecture
        diagrams, technology selection rationale, and implementation
        specifications.

    (c) Edge-Deployed ML Integration Framework
        Develop a framework for integrating machine learning models into
        industrial edge devices at customer sites, including model serving
        infrastructure on industrial PCs, monitoring dashboards, A/B testing
        capabilities, and rollback procedures. The framework shall support
        both cloud-hosted model training and edge-deployed inference for
        predictive maintenance and anomaly detection use cases.

    (d) Knowledge Transfer and Training Program
        Design and deliver a training program for the Client's internal
        engineering team (estimated 24 engineers), consisting of no fewer than
        40 hours of instructor-led training sessions, hands-on workshops, and
        written documentation. The program shall cover all architectures and
        frameworks developed under this Agreement.

                    ARTICLE 4: CONFIDENTIALITY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

4.1 Definition. "Confidential Information" means all non-public information
    disclosed by one Party ("Disclosing Party") to the other Party ("Receiving
    Party") in connection with this Agreement, whether disclosed orally, in
    writing, electronically, or by inspection, including but not limited to:
    trade secrets, business plans, financial data, customer lists, technical
    specifications, source code, algorithms, proprietary methodologies,
    industrial process parameters, and client facility data.

4.2 Obligations. The Receiving Party shall:
    (a) hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence;
    (b) not disclose Confidential Information to any third party without the
        prior written consent of the Disclosing Party;
    (c) use Confidential Information solely for the purposes of performing or
        receiving the Services under this Agreement;
    (d) protect Confidential Information using at least the same degree of care
        it uses to protect its own confidential information, but in no event
        less than reasonable care;
    (e) limit access to Confidential Information to those employees and
        subcontractors who have a need to know and who are bound by
        confidentiality obligations at least as restrictive as those contained
        herein.

4.3 Exceptions. The confidentiality obligations shall not apply to information
    that: (a) is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the
    Receiving Party; (b) was known to the Receiving Party prior to disclosure;
    (c) is independently developed by the Receiving Party; or (d) is required
    to be disclosed by law, regulation, or court order, provided that the
    Receiving Party gives prompt notice to the Disclosing Party.

4.4 Duration. The confidentiality obligations under this Article shall survive
    the termination or expiration of this Agreement for a period of three (3)
    years.

                    ARTICLE 5: INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

5.1 Pre-Existing IP. Each Party shall retain all rights in its pre-existing

    Work Product.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
                    ARTICLE 6: TERMINATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

6.1 Termination for Convenience. Either Party may terminate this Agreement for
    any reason by providing ninety (90) days' written notice to the other Party.

6.2 Termination for Cause. Either Party may terminate this Agreement
    immediately upon written notice if:
    (a) the other Party commits a material breach of this Agreement and fails
        to cure such breach within thirty (30) days of receiving written notice
        thereof;
    (b) the other Party becomes insolvent, files for bankruptcy, or has a
        receiver appointed for a substantial part of its assets;
    (c) the other Party is unable to perform its obligations due to force
        majeure for a continuous period exceeding sixty (60) days.

6.3 Effect of Termination. Upon termination:
    (a) the Consultant shall deliver to the Client all completed and
        in-progress Work Product;
    (b) the Client shall pay the Consultant for all Services performed and
        expenses incurred through the effective date of termination;
    (c) the provisions of Articles 4 (Confidentiality), 5 (Intellectual
        Property), 7 (Liability), and 8 (Governing Law) shall survive
        termination.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
                    ARTICLE 7: LIABILITY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

7.1 The Consultant's total aggregate liability under this Agreement shall not
    exceed the total fees actually paid by the Client to the Consultant during
    the twelve (12) months preceding the event giving rise to the claim.

7.2 Neither Party shall be liable for any indirect, incidental, consequential,
    special, or punitive damages, including loss of profits, loss of data, or
    business interruption, regardless of the form of action.

7.3 The limitations set forth in this Article 7 shall not apply to:
    (a) breaches of Article 4 (Confidentiality); (b) willful misconduct or
    gross negligence; or (c) liability that cannot be limited under applicable
    law.
