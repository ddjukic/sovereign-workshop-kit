                        EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
================================================================================

AlpenTech GmbH ("AlpenTech" or the "Company") reports strong financial
performance for the third quarter of 2025, with consolidated revenue reaching
EUR 12.4 million, representing a 15.8% increase compared to Q3 2024 (EUR 10.7
million). This growth was primarily driven by continued momentum in our
Industrial Automation segment and the successful expansion of our Predictive
Maintenance offering to new manufacturing clients across the DACH region.

EBITDA for Q3 2025 reached EUR 2.6 million, corresponding to an EBITDA margin
of 21.0%, compared to EUR 2.1 million (19.6% margin) in Q3 2024. The
improvement in profitability reflects the Company's disciplined cost management,
increased recurring revenue share from long-term maintenance contracts, and the
operational leverage achieved through our engineering platform standardization
initiative completed in Q2 2025.

Net income for the quarter was EUR 1.5 million (EUR 1.2 million in Q3 2024),
resulting in a net margin of 12.1% (11.2% in Q3 2024).

Cash and cash equivalents stood at EUR 8.9 million as of September 30, 2025,
compared to EUR 6.7 million at the end of Q3 2024, providing a solid foundation
for planned investments in R&D capabilities and regional expansion.

                    REVENUE BREAKDOWN BY SEGMENT
================================================================================

The Company operates through three reportable business segments:

1. INDUSTRIAL AUTOMATION & ENGINEERING CONSULTING
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Revenue Q3 2025:            EUR 5.6 million
Revenue Q3 2024:            EUR 4.5 million
Year-over-Year Growth:      +24.4%
Share of Total Revenue:     45.2%

The Industrial Automation segment continues to be our primary growth engine.
Revenue growth was fueled by the onboarding of 12 new enterprise clients during
the quarter, bringing the total active customer count to 87. The segment
benefits from the ongoing Industrie 4.0 modernization wave across Austrian and
German manufacturing firms, with particular strength in automotive supplier
and steel processing verticals.

Key wins during the quarter included a three-year framework agreement with
Voestalpine Stahl GmbH valued at EUR 1.8 million and an expansion of our
engagement with BMW Werk Steyr worth EUR 720,000 annually. Additionally,
a new engagement with Rosenbauer International AG was signed for EUR 430,000
(annual), managed by account lead DI Maria Holzinger (employee ID: AT-EMP-0078,
personal mobile: +43 664 519 3847, maria.holzinger@alpentech.at).

2. PREDICTIVE MAINTENANCE & R&D
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Revenue Q3 2025:            EUR 4.2 million
Revenue Q3 2024:            EUR 3.5 million
Year-over-Year Growth:      +20.0%
Share of Total Revenue:     33.9%

The Predictive Maintenance & R&D segment delivered strong performance,
benefiting from increasing demand for condition monitoring and failure
prediction solutions in heavy industry. The segment's growth was supported
by the launch of our "AlpenPredict Edge" product in July 2025, which deploys
ML models directly on industrial edge gateways, enabling real-time anomaly
detection without cloud dependency. The product has already generated
EUR 850,000 in revenue during its first quarter.

The segment completed 9 new implementation projects during Q3, with an average
project value of EUR 310,000. The implementation backlog stood at EUR 5.7
million as of September 30, 2025, representing approximately 5.1 months of
revenue visibility.

3. ENGINEERING CONSULTING & PROFESSIONAL SERVICES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Revenue Q3 2025:            EUR 2.6 million
Revenue Q3 2024:            EUR 2.7 million
Year-over-Year Growth:      -3.7%
Share of Total Revenue:     20.9%

The Engineering Consulting segment experienced a slight planned decline as the
Company continues its strategic shift toward higher-margin product-based
revenue models. Several large consulting engagements with Siemens Austria
concluded in Q2 2025, and the deliberate reduction in low-margin staff
augmentation projects contributed to the revenue decrease.

Despite lower revenue, the segment's gross margin improved to 39.4% from 32.1%
in Q3 2024, as the Company focused on higher-value advisory, commissioning,
and architecture engagements. The average daily rate for professional services
increased by 12% year-over-year to EUR 1,720.

                          COST ANALYSIS
================================================================================

Total operating costs for Q3 2025 amounted to EUR 9.8 million, compared to
EUR 8.6 million in Q3 2024 (+14.0%). The increase in absolute costs was more
than offset by revenue growth, resulting in improved operating leverage.

COST OF REVENUE:                    EUR 5.9 million (Q3 2024: EUR 5.3 million)
    - Personnel costs (engineers):  EUR 3.6 million (+9.1% YoY)
    - Subcontractor costs:          EUR 1.1 million (+15.8% YoY)
    - Equipment & materials:        EUR 0.8 million (+14.3% YoY)
    - Third-party licenses:         EUR 0.4 million (+0.0% YoY)

Gross profit reached EUR 6.5 million (52.4% margin), up from EUR 5.4 million
(50.5% margin) in Q3 2024.

OPERATING EXPENSES:                 EUR 3.9 million (Q3 2024: EUR 3.3 million)
    - Research & Development:       EUR 1.9 million (15.3% of revenue)
    - Sales & Business Dev:         EUR 1.3 million (10.5% of revenue)
    - General & Administrative:     EUR 0.7 million (5.6% of revenue)

R&D investment increased by 18.8% year-over-year, reflecting the Company's
commitment to product innovation, particularly in edge-deployed ML models
and digital twin capabilities. The R&D team expanded to 38 engineers, up from
31 in Q3 2024.

Sales & Business Development expenditure grew by 13.0%, primarily due to the
expansion of the sales team into the German market with a new representative
office in Munich, opened in August 2025.

General & Administrative costs remained well-controlled at EUR 0.7 million,
flat compared to Q3 2024, despite the Company's overall growth.

================================================================================
                          RISK FACTORS
================================================================================

The Company has identified the following key risks that may affect future
performance:

1. TALENT ACQUISITION AND RETENTION
   The competitive labor market for skilled automation engineers, data
   scientists, and embedded systems specialists in the DACH region continues
   to present challenges. The Company's average time-to-fill for technical
   positions increased to 72 days in Q3 2025, up from 58 days in Q3 2024.
   Wage inflation for technical roles averaged 7.2% year-over-year. Failure
   to attract and retain key talent could constrain growth and impact service
   quality.

2. CLIENT CONCENTRATION RISK
   The Company's five largest clients represent approximately 38% of total
   revenue. While the Company is actively diversifying its client base, a
   loss or significant reduction in engagement scope with a major client
   could materially impact financial performance. The Company has set a
   target to reduce top-5 client concentration to below 30% by Q4 2026.

3. REGULATORY AND COMPLIANCE RISK
   The evolving European regulatory landscape, including the EU AI Act, the
   Machinery Regulation 2023/1230, and updated GDPR enforcement guidance,
   requires ongoing investment in compliance infrastructure. The Company
   estimates incremental compliance costs of EUR 250,000 to EUR 400,000
   annually beginning in 2026, particularly for AI-enabled predictive
   maintenance systems classified as high-risk under the EU AI Act.

4. MACROECONOMIC UNCERTAINTY
   Continued economic uncertainty in the Eurozone, including potential
   recessionary pressures in key markets (Germany, Austria), could lead to
   delays in customer purchasing decisions, extended sales cycles, and
   increased price sensitivity. The manufacturing sector, which represents
   approximately 72% of the Company's customer base, is particularly sensitive
   to economic cycles.
